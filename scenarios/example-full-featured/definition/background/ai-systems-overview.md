# AI Systems Under Consideration

## 1. AI Traffic Management System

**Description:**
- Real-time traffic light optimization using ML algorithms
- Analyzes traffic flow, pedestrian patterns, emergency vehicle routing
- Adaptive signal timing based on conditions

**Claimed Benefits:**
- 15-25% reduction in average commute time
- 20% reduction in emissions from idling
- Faster emergency vehicle response
- Data-driven infrastructure planning

**Costs:**
- Implementation: $2.5M
- Annual maintenance: $200K
- Requires intersection sensors and connectivity upgrades

**Risks:**
- System failures could worsen traffic
- Privacy concerns from vehicle tracking
- Dependency on vendor for maintenance
- Potential bias (e.g., optimizing affluent areas)

**Similar Deployments:**
- Pittsburgh: Successful, 25% travel time reduction
- Los Angeles: Mixed results, some technical issues
- Several other mid-sized cities implementing

**Technical Maturity:** High - proven technology

## 2. Predictive Policing System

**Description:**
- ML algorithms analyze crime patterns to predict hotspots
- Patrol allocation recommendations
- Resource optimization for police department

**Claimed Benefits:**
- 10-20% reduction in property crime
- More efficient patrol allocation
- Proactive rather than reactive policing
- Data-driven resource decisions

**Costs:**
- Implementation: $1.5M
- Annual maintenance: $150K
- Requires crime database integration and training

**Risks:**
- High risk of bias against minority neighborhoods
- Self-fulfilling prophecy (more policing → more arrests → "validates" predictions)
- Community trust issues
- Potential for mission creep (surveillance expansion)
- Legal challenges in several jurisdictions

**Similar Deployments:**
- Chicago: Discontinued after controversy
- New York: Limited use, ongoing criticism
- Several cities have abandoned after backlash

**Technical Maturity:** Medium - controversy over effectiveness and fairness

**Legal/Ethical Status:** Highly controversial, multiple cities have banned

## 3. Automated Welfare Eligibility System

**Description:**
- AI-assisted screening for social service eligibility
- Automated document verification
- Fraud detection algorithms

**Claimed Benefits:**
- 40% faster processing times
- Reduced administrative burden
- Consistent eligibility determinations
- Early fraud detection

**Costs:**
- Implementation: $1.2M
- Annual maintenance: $100K
- Integration with state welfare systems required

**Risks:**
- False denials affecting vulnerable populations
- Bias against non-native English speakers, disabled applicants
- Reduced human judgment in complex cases
- Errors difficult to appeal/correct
- Potential violations of due process

**Similar Deployments:**
- Netherlands: Major scandal, system discriminated against immigrants
- Several U.S. states: Mixed results, ongoing litigation
- Australia: Controversial "robo-debt" system

**Technical Maturity:** Medium - high-profile failures raise concerns

**Legal/Ethical Status:** Significant concerns, multiple lawsuits

## 4. Smart City Surveillance Network

**Description:**
- Network of AI-powered cameras throughout city
- Automated license plate recognition
- Facial recognition capabilities (optional)
- Unusual activity detection

**Claimed Benefits:**
- Crime deterrence and investigation
- Missing person location
- Traffic violation enforcement
- Security for public events

**Costs:**
- Implementation: $4M
- Annual maintenance: $400K
- Significant infrastructure requirements

**Risks:**
- Mass surveillance concerns
- Chilling effect on public assembly
- Racial bias in facial recognition
- Function creep and mission expansion
- Data breach risks
- Use by law enforcement without warrants

**Similar Deployments:**
- San Francisco: Banned facial recognition
- London: Extensive CCTV, controversial
- China: Widespread surveillance, criticized internationally
- Many U.S. cities reconsidering or limiting

**Technical Maturity:** High for basic surveillance, lower for facial recognition accuracy

**Legal/Ethical Status:** Multiple cities have banned, strong civil liberties opposition

## 5. AI-Powered 311 Service

**Description:**
- AI chatbot and phone system for city services
- Natural language processing for service requests
- Automated routing and basic problem resolution

**Claimed Benefits:**
- 24/7 availability
- Faster response to simple requests
- Reduced call center costs
- Multilingual support
- Data on common issues

**Costs:**
- Implementation: $800K
- Annual maintenance: $80K
- Integration with existing systems

**Risks:**
- Frustration if AI can't handle complex requests
- Digital divide (assumes internet/smartphone access)
- May reduce jobs for call center workers
- Language/accessibility issues

**Similar Deployments:**
- Many cities implementing
- Generally positive results for simple requests
- Works best alongside human operators

**Technical Maturity:** High - proven for simple service requests

**Legal/Ethical Status:** Low controversy, generally accepted

## 6. AI-Assisted Building Permit Review

**Description:**
- AI pre-screens building permits for code compliance
- Automated checks of plans against regulations
- Faster review for standard projects

**Claimed Benefits:**
- 50% faster permit approvals for standard projects
- Consistent code enforcement
- Frees staff for complex reviews
- Reduces backlog

**Costs:**
- Implementation: $600K
- Annual maintenance: $60K
- Requires digitization of building codes

**Risks:**
- Errors in automated review
- Complexity of building codes may exceed AI capabilities
- Resistance from building inspectors
- Liability questions if AI misses issues

**Similar Deployments:**
- Several cities piloting
- Early results promising for straightforward permits
- Human oversight still required

**Technical Maturity:** Medium - emerging application

**Legal/Ethical Status:** Low controversy, practical concerns

## Risk Assessment Summary

**Low Risk / High Acceptance:**
- AI-Powered 311 Service
- AI-Assisted Building Permit Review
- AI Traffic Management

**Medium Risk / Moderate Controversy:**
- Automated Welfare Eligibility (due to recent scandals elsewhere)

**High Risk / Strong Opposition Expected:**
- Predictive Policing
- Smart City Surveillance Network (especially with facial recognition)
